Bio

Joyce Rice is a designer and programmer with a focus in journalism and education. She makes websites, games, and other digital products to help newsrooms and educators tell powerful stories.

Her work has been written about by Newsweek, Fast Company, Citylab, and others. She has spoken about media innovation and collaboration at SXSW, Game Developers Conference, on The Verge's Top Shelf, and elsewhere. Her past clients include PBS, Vox Media, Bed Bath & Beyond, and more.

She is proficient with the basic webstack (HTML/CSS/Javascript) and up-to-date with web standards and best practices. She is also comfortable with a number of web libraries and technologies, including WordPress, D3, and jQuery. She is a quick study on new software and happy to research and learn the best tool for the job.

In her spare time, Joyce writes Python for her Raspberry Pi and explores the great swamps of Florida.

Joyce is also a cartoonist, you can find her comics here. During the summers, she is a lecturer at California College of the Arts where she teaches other cartoonists the basic webstack so they can share their own digital stories.

She is currently accepting new clients and is available for speaking engagements. If you'd like to work together, get in touch with more information about your project!
